Topic Area

Joseph Lister & Sterilisation

How one surgeon's application of germ theory transformed the operating theatre — and made modern surgery possible.

Available Sources

📄
Source 1

Joseph Lister and the Development of Antiseptic Surgery

A scholarly account of Lister's work at Glasgow Royal Infirmary and Edinburgh, the scientific basis of his antiseptic technique, the reception of his ideas by the medical community, and his lasting influence on surgical practice and infection control.
📰
Source 2

The Man Who Made Surgery Safe: Joseph Lister's Germ-Fighting Revolution

A popular science feature presenting Lister as a lone visionary who transformed medicine against resistance. Engaging narrative style with some simplification of the science and historical context.
✍️
Source 3

Lister, Carbolic Acid, and Why We're Still Poisoning Ourselves

A wellness blog post that celebrates Lister's "natural" instincts while arguing that modern antiseptics and sterilisation chemicals are harmful to health. Loosely based on history, with significant factual drift.
🏢
Source 4

Joseph Lister and the Birth of Sterile Surgery — The CleanMed NZ Story of Clean

A history of Lister's work published by a surgical supplies company. The facts are largely accurate, but the article exists to build credibility for the company's product range — Lister's legacy is the backdrop, not the subject.
🌿
Source 5

What Joseph Lister Really Taught Us — And Why Modern Medicine Buried the Lesson

An alternative health article that opens with accurate history before arguing that Lister was inspired by nature, that modern antiseptics are "toxic," and that essential oils carry on his real legacy. Uses loaded language and omits counter-evidence.
