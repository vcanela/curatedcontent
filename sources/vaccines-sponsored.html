What Parents Should Know About Vaccine Ingredients (And How to Support Your Child's Natural Immunity)

Most of us follow our vaccination schedules without a second thought. But as a parent, it's natural to want to understand what's actually going into your child's body — and what steps you can take to give their immune system the best possible foundation.

Supporting your child's immunity is one of the most important things you can do as a parent.

When your child's plunket nurse hands you the vaccination schedule, it can feel overwhelming. There are so many doses, so many different vaccines — and the information sheet that comes with each one is dense, technical, and hard to interpret if you don't have a medical background.

We believe parents deserve clear, honest information. So in this article, we're going to walk through some of the common ingredients found in childhood vaccines, explain what they're used for, and then share our philosophy on how nutritional support can complement your child's immune health journey.

Common Vaccine Ingredients: What You Need to Know

Vaccines are complex biological products. Beyond the active component — the antigen that trains your child's immune system to recognise a specific pathogen — they also contain a range of other substances that serve various manufacturing and delivery functions.

Aluminium Adjuvants

One of the most commonly used additives in vaccines is aluminium salts, often aluminium hydroxide or aluminium phosphate. These are known as "adjuvants" — substances that boost the immune response, essentially helping the vaccine work harder so that less antigen is needed per dose.

Worth Knowing

Aluminium is the most abundant metal in the Earth's crust, and humans are exposed to it daily through food, water, and cookware. However, the aluminium in vaccines is injected directly into muscle tissue rather than ingested, which means it enters the body through a different pathway.

While health authorities maintain that the amounts used are safe, some researchers have called for more long-term studies on cumulative exposure across the full childhood immunisation schedule — particularly for children who may be receiving multiple vaccines at the same appointment.

Formaldehyde

Formaldehyde is used during vaccine manufacturing to inactivate viruses and bacteria, ensuring they cannot cause disease. Most of it is removed during purification, but trace amounts can remain in the final product. The human body actually produces formaldehyde naturally as part of normal metabolism — at levels significantly higher than the trace amounts found in vaccines. Nonetheless, some parents find it unsettling to see the word "formaldehyde" on an ingredient list.

Preservatives: Thimerosal

Thimerosal, a mercury-based preservative, was historically used in multi-dose vaccine vials to prevent bacterial contamination. It is now absent from most routine childhood vaccines in New Zealand, though it remains in some multi-dose flu vaccine formulations. The type of mercury in thimerosal (ethylmercury) is processed and eliminated by the body differently from the methylmercury found in fish — though this distinction is not always well communicated to parents.

Polysorbate 80 and Other Stabilisers

Polysorbate 80 is an emulsifier used in many foods, cosmetics, and medicines. In vaccines, it helps stabilise the product and keep ingredients in suspension. It is considered safe at the quantities used, but as with other additives, it is an ingredient derived from synthetic chemistry rather than natural sources.

"Being informed about what goes into your child's body is not anti-science. It's good parenting."

Does This Mean Vaccines Are Unsafe?

We want to be clear: PureLife Wellness NZ supports the childhood immunisation schedule. Vaccines have been one of the most significant achievements in public health history, dramatically reducing childhood mortality from diseases like measles, polio, whooping cough, and diphtheria.

The purpose of this article is not to discourage vaccination. It is to acknowledge that informed parents ask questions — and that curiosity about what's in a vaccine is not the same as refusing one.

What we do believe is that the nutritional and immune foundation your child has going into their vaccination appointments can influence how well their immune system mounts a response — and how quickly they recover from any post-vaccination tiredness or discomfort.

Supporting Your Child's Natural Immunity

The immune system is not a single organ — it's a complex, whole-body network that depends on good nutrition, sleep, gut health, and stress levels to function optimally. Children whose diets are high in processed foods, low in vegetables, and short on key micronutrients may mount a less robust immune response than children with excellent nutritional status.

Key nutrients that support immune function in children include:

  • Vitamin D: Critical for immune modulation. Many New Zealand children have suboptimal vitamin D levels, particularly during winter months.
  • Zinc: Essential for the development and function of immune cells, including the T-cells that vaccines train.
  • Vitamin C: Supports both the innate and adaptive immune systems and has antioxidant properties.
  • Probiotics and prebiotics: The gut houses approximately 70% of the immune system. A healthy microbiome supports effective immune responses.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids: Play a role in regulating inflammation, which is part of a healthy immune response.

Timing: Should You Give Supplements Around Vaccination Appointments?

This is a question we get asked frequently. Some integrative health practitioners suggest ensuring your child's nutritional status is optimised in the weeks before and after vaccination — not to interfere with the immune response, but to ensure the body has the raw materials it needs to build immunity effectively.

We always recommend discussing any supplementation with your healthcare provider. That said, ensuring your child is eating well, sleeping adequately, and not under unusual stress in the days around their vaccination appointment is simply good health practice — and something most parents would agree with regardless of their views on vaccines.

Gut Health and the Immune System

One area that is increasingly supported by research is the connection between gut microbiome health and immune function. A diverse, thriving gut microbiome appears to support a more balanced immune response — both in terms of the initial reaction to pathogens (or vaccine antigens) and in terms of the regulatory processes that prevent the immune system from overreacting.

Antibiotics, processed foods, and high-sugar diets can all negatively affect the microbiome — and many modern children are exposed to all three. This is where targeted probiotic support can play a role.
